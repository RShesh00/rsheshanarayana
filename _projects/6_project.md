Classical force fields fail to capture the complex, ion-specific behavior of aqueous electrolytes in nanoscale confinement - a regime critical to energy storage and membrane separations. This MS thesis project trained **DFT-accurate neural network potentials (NNPs)** via active learning on HPC clusters, achieving force RMSEs ≤ 0.07 eV/Å while enabling nanosecond-scale MD simulations.

Simulations revealed stark ion-specific behavior: K⁺ exhibited 3× stronger interfacial adsorption and 2× faster diffusion than Na⁺ in confined geometries - trends entirely absent in classical force fields. These findings highlight the necessity of quantum-accurate potentials for understanding nanoconfinement effects.
