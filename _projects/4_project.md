Retrosynthesis — predicting the synthetic precursors of a target molecule — is a core challenge in computer-aided drug design. Transformer-based models have shown promise, but their training is sensitive to reaction complexity. This project introduces a **curriculum learning framework** that progressively exposes the model to more complex reactions during training, mimicking how chemists develop expertise.

The approach significantly improves generalization to unseen reaction types and low-resource settings, demonstrating that training curricula — not just architectures — are a first-class lever for improving reaction prediction.

*Published in **J. Chem. Inf. Model.** (2025, Vol. 65, No. 20) — Supplementary Journal Cover.*
