Understanding catalytic CO₂ hydrogenation at scale requires navigating thousands of elementary reactions - far beyond what manual DFT studies can cover. This collaborative project combines **extensive DFT calculations, ML-predicted activation barriers, and automated kinetic modeling** to construct and analyze a massive reaction network for CO₂ reduction on copper.

Starting from 152 elementary reactions, the framework expands to 9,389 reactions, achieving 40-fold higher predicted CO₂ conversion rates consistent with experimental methanol and CO selectivity trends. The work reveals new mechanistic pathways invisible to smaller-scale analyses.

*Under review at **Nature Communications** (2025).*
