Molecular representation learning has transformed computational chemistry by replacing hand-crafted descriptors with learned, task-adaptive embeddings. This project surveys the field across domains - from drug discovery and materials science to reaction prediction and de novo design - tracing the evolution from graph neural networks and autoencoders to **diffusion models, transformers, and contrastive self-supervised frameworks**.

The review critically evaluates physically consistent embeddings, multi-modal fusion strategies, and generalization limits, and charts open frontiers including molecular foundation models, equivariant architectures, and low-data regimes.
